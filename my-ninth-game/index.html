Guess What

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    It's all a fun game of chance! Roll the die to see if you get to pull a card off the board. If you can, then test your luck with pulling your correct color for points! I think that the amount of randomness in the game is unique. There isn't a lot of strategy, so it makes it more of fun/goofy game to able to play and laugh with friends about. The game is much more about luck than anything else and I think that tends to be something you don't see as often in digital or analog games.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    The game keeps track of lots of stuff! Once the game is started, it keeps track of player's points and who's turn it is. It is able to correct tell you which player's turn it is even when a turn is being skipped. The cover's over the square disappear as you drag them off so that the board is kept nice and orderly. The dice as a nice sound going along with it to make it more realistic, the random number is generate within the roll and shown on the screen. Currently, the computer needs you to indicate what was underneath the cover, but I'm working on that being automatic in the game.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    I learned a lot about keeping track of scores. It was difficult to keep up with all the combinations that could happen, when the score changed, and when the player's turns changed as well. I had a rule in the analog game that when you rolled a 6 you could steal a point from the other player if they had any, but I had a very difficult time trying to figure out how to incorporate that so the button wasn't available constantly. Also that you got the option to choose whether to do that or take another cover from the board. That's definitely what I will be working on for the next iteraiton. That and trying to make it so you can play against the computer.
