Crazy Cards

  • Originality: What is original about your game?

    • The game is more about chance than anything else. You pull the covers off the cards and hope that you either find the green card or find a blue card. It's just a lucky guess for what might be under the card and I think that adds some originality to the game.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?

    • There is a function that removes the covers from the cards underneath so that the covers are interactive. The computer also counts how many tries you have left and also keeps track of where all the blue cards and the winning star card are. When you pull a cover off the game checks that location against all other possible locations to decide if you have a winning card, an extra try, or you've used up a try. Once refresh, the game also reshuffles all the cards so you get a unique layout for the next game.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?

    • I used a lot that I had been learning from my last game to create an easier to play, random game. All the cards are added through an array structure and reshuffled every game. Making sure that the correct card coordinates were being recorded and also checked at the right time was hard. It was difficult also to figure out a way to make the game a little more fun to play than just pulling random cards. I added the blue cards giving you extra turns so that the player has more of a chance to actually win the game.
